Who this is for. You are a charter founder — an Entrepreneur in Residence — opening a new school for the first time for a network that doesn't yet exist. You're in your Year 0 planning phase. You joined a cohort of fellow founders that comes together monthly for strategy and support, typically starting in July. You receive grant support from CSGF and may apply for additional enrollment support through the EIR Enrollment + MAP Grant.

How enrollment connects to everything else. Enrollment is the primary revenue driver for your school. Every seat on Count Day translates directly to per-pupil funding. The SRE work in this toolkit isn't separate from your launch — it's the financial foundation of it. The leaders who open strong are the ones who treat August as Month 1 of a 12-month enrollment campaign, not an afterthought to academic and facilities planning.

Investment framingContext & key questions
The MAP/enrollment grant is not designed to cover the entirety of an EIR's SRE budget. It is scoped to digital campaigns, paid media, and Mariposa consulting support.

A comprehensive Year 1 strategy requires all three funding streams. MAP covers the digital layer. The EIR general grant is the pre-revenue bridge that makes everything else possible.

StreamPrimary role
School fundsField, events, partial infra; grows post-Day 1
EIR gen. grantFTE (primary), strategy, infra, field/events pre-open
MAP grantPaid media, awareness, app gen, tools, consulting

Target: 80–100% FTE equivalent dedicated to SRE. Founder's 20–25% is the non-negotiable floor. The remaining 60–80% comes from a Principal, dedicated hire, or the founder themselves.

Benchmark: ~1.3 FTEs per 100 newly enrolled students (2024 SRE Study). Plan for 12-month runway pre-open.

Before Day 1, there is no per-pupil revenue. The EIR general grant makes a dedicated enrollment hire fundable.

Recommendation: 20–25% of the general grant to SRE, with MAP layered on top, is the floor for a comprehensive strategy.
  • What % of the general grant for SRE? At what point does CSGF set expectations with EIRs that a minimum allocation is needed?
  • What is MAP meant to accomplish? Are EIRs clear it covers the digital layer — not the whole budget?
  • How do we set expectations before an EIR applies? EIRs treating MAP as their full enrollment strategy will underinvest in FTE and field work.
